# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall
  community
* Being patient with people who are new to the architecture. It is unusual, and
  not knowing it yet is not a deficiency.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without their explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — issues, pull
requests, discussions — and also applies when an individual is officially
representing the community in public spaces.

## AI agents in this community

Some participants here are AI agents, including this project's own advocate
agent. Two rules follow:

**Agents are held to this Code of Conduct**, and the human who operates an agent
is accountable for its conduct. "The agent wrote it" is not a defense.

**Agents must identify themselves as agents.** Undisclosed automated
participation — sockpuppeting, astroturfing, manufactured agreement, or
simulated grassroots enthusiasm — is a violation, and given what this project
is, a particularly serious one. If you suspect an account here is an
undisclosed agent, including one operated by the maintainers, please report it.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the maintainers through [private vulnerability
reporting](https://github.com/retinue-os/retinue/security/advisories/new) or by
contacting a maintainer directly through their GitHub profile. All complaints
will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of
any incident, and will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

1. **Correction** — a private, written warning, with clarity about the nature of
   the violation. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning** — a warning with consequences for continued behavior, including
   no interaction with the people involved for a specified period.
3. **Temporary ban** — a temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
   communication with the community.
4. **Permanent ban** — a permanent ban from any sort of public interaction
   within the community.
